Business Insights & Recommendations
Primary Driver · Contract Type
Month-to-Month Plans Are a Churn Waiting Room
16.02%
Monthly contract churn rate — 7× higher than annual
Customers on flexible plans face zero financial friction to switch providers. The absence of contractual commitment is the single strongest behavioral predictor of churn in this dataset.
💡 Commitment Credit: Offer a 10% discount for 90 days to monthly customers who upgrade to a 12-month agreement at their 90-day anniversary.
Temporal Risk · Tenure Analysis
First Two Years Are the Critical Retention Window
10.64%
12–24 month segment churn rate — highest of all tenure groups
Risk peaks at 12–24 months, closely followed by the <12 month new customer segment. Once a customer crosses 24 months, churn drops to 9.58%. The "settling period" is where we lose the most customers.
💡 First-Year Milestone: Trigger automated outreach at months 6 and 18 with personalized service upgrades and loyalty incentives.
Technology Segment · Internet Service
Fiber Optic Customers Are Price-Sensitive
#1
Internet Service is the most important permutation feature (0.0456)
Fiber Optic service delivers superior speeds but at higher monthly costs, increasing price sensitivity versus DSL customers. Premium service creates premium churn risk when value perception erodes.
💡 Fiber Value-Lock: 15% service credit for 6 months to Fiber customers whose monthly charges exceed the company average of $67.33.
Model Limitation · Decision Support
High Accuracy Hides a Critical Business Failure
91.6%
Churners the model fails to identify — an unacceptable miss rate
86.94% accuracy sounds impressive until you realize the model misses over 91% of actual churners. In retention strategy, false negatives are costly — every missed churner is revenue permanently lost.
💡 Next Step: Deploy XGBoost with class imbalance handling (scale_pos_weight) and recall-optimized threshold tuning to actually surface at-risk customers.
